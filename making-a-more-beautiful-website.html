    Making a more beautiful website

    In my previous post I grumbled about the limited site-editing features of the Publii framework.

    I said: "this site isn't exactly the most beautiful thing in existence, but it's hard to improve its looks."

    On reflection, it's probably not Publii's fault, but mine. After all, I'm the designer, however amateur. All design must work within constraints of some kind, and good design makes use of the inevitable constraints to create a beautiful, pleasing or useful outcome.

    So I had another try.

    If in doubt, simplify, said someone, somewhere, once. So I took out the AI-generated header image and removed most of the colour. That has certainly helped to improve the look of the site. A few additional tweaks and it's better already.
