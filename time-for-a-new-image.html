    Time for a new image

    I’ve changed the photo at the top of this page. The button-eyed monkey has earned some rest, and is replaced by the AI smiley. This is an image generated by the IA art app @midjourney. This particular image was generated by @ctbeiser using only the prompt:

    hey, hows it going? | quizical look | friendly person | leica artstation HDR | extremely detailed | seed=da47c18ff

    See the context at https://twitter.com/ctbeiser/status/1514120783873798144

    AI art is coming on in leaps and bounds, but midjourney is the first exeplar that has really made me sit up and take notice. In my opinion, it’s creating a whole new way of looking at the world, which is what all good art does.

    AI art doesn’t really put human artists out of a job, any more than acrylic paint did, or synthetic paintbrushes, or photography. It just makes everyone anxious at first, then becomes just another tool for humans to make use of.

    A while ago I quoted this:

    “We must get used to being less clever than the artificial brain that we have produced, just as our teeth are less strong than a millstone and our ability to fly negligible compared with that of a jet aircraft.”

    • André Leroi-Gourhan, Gesture and Speech. English translation, Cambridge: MIT Press, 1993, p.265.

    Already there are digital artists making AI-assisted art. It’s a trend that will keep getting stronger, and I think we’ve only just begun to see what’s possible.
